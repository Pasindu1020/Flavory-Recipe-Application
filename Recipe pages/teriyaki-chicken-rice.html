Teriyaki Chicken Rice Bowl

The Teriyaki Chicken Rice Bowl is a flavorful and comforting dish made with tender chicken thighs or breasts cooked in a rich, savory-sweet teriyaki sauce. Served over a bed of warm steamed rice and paired with crisp sautéed vegetables like broccoli, carrots, and bell peppers, this bowl brings together balance and bold taste. The glossy teriyaki glaze—made from soy sauce, ginger, garlic, sugar, and mirin—coats each piece of chicken perfectly, creating a satisfying blend of umami and sweetness. Finished with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and fresh green onions, this dish is perfect for a wholesome lunch or dinner that feels both hearty and healthy.

Prep Time

20 mins

Cook Time

20 mins

Total Time

40 mins

Ingredients

Garlic Soy Chicken

  • 1 lb. Chicken thighs
  • 1 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Rice vinegar
  • 1 Garlic clove
  • Black pepper to taste

Teriyaki Sauce

  • 1/3 cup Soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup Chicken broth
  • 3 tbsp Rice vinegar
  • 3 tbsp White sugar
  • 1 Garlic clove (large)
  • 1/2 tsp Red pepper flakes
  • 1/4 tsp White pepper
  • 1/2 tbsp Corn starch
  • 1 tbsp Water

Vegetables

  • 2–3 tbsp Avocado oil
  • 1 Red bell pepper
  • 1 cup Edamame
  • Salt (to taste)

Carrot Salad

  • 1 Carrot (medium/large)
  • 1 Garlic clove
  • 2 tbsp Rice vinegar
  • 2 tsp Sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Sugar
  • Black Pepper (to taste)

Other Items & Toppings

  • 3–4 cups Jasmine rice (steamed)
  • 10 Wonton sheets
  • Black & white sesame seeds
  • Green onion
  • Kewpie mayo

Directions

  1. Make Carrot salad by peeling the skin of a carrot. Grate the carrot or cut into julienne slices. Season with grated garlic, rice vinegar, sesame oil, salt, sugar, and black pepper.
  2. Cut up chicken into bite-sized pieces and season with soy sauce, rice vinegar, grated garlic, and black pepper. Let sit for 10–15 minutes while you prep the vegetables and sauce.
  3. Make teriyaki sauce by combining soy sauce, chicken broth, rice vinegar, white sugar, grated garlic, red pepper flakes, and white pepper. Whisk to combine. In a separate small bowl, combine cornstarch with cold water and stir well.
  4. Bring a small pot of water to boil, add in edamame, and cook for 4–5 minutes until desired doneness. Drain all excess water from edamame.
  5. Pour a good amount of oil into a wok and heat on medium. Slice small wonton wrappers into strips and fry in the hot oil for 1–2 minutes until golden. Transfer to a plate lined with a paper towel to absorb excess oil and lightly salt.
  6. Drain excess oil from wok and place on high heat. Pour in avocado oil and stir-fry chopped bell pepper and edamame for 1–2 minutes or until desired doneness. Lightly season with salt.
  7. Stir-fry chicken in the same wok on high-medium heat until chicken takes on color. Transfer chicken to plate.
  8. In the same wok, pour in teriyaki sauce and simmer for 2–3 minutes on medium heat making sure to scrape any excess flavor on the bottom of the pan.
  9. Pour the starch slurry into the teriyaki sauce. Allow to simmer for an additional minute or until sauce thickens slightly.
  10. Make a Kewpie mayo sauce by combining 1/2 cup Kewpie mayo with 1–3 tbsp water and mix well to combine. Adjust water amount depending on the viscosity preferred for the sauce.
  11. Plate the garlic soy chicken, stir-fried veggies, carrot salad, fried wontons, and green onions over warm jasmine rice. Sprinkle white and black sesame seeds over the veggies and drizzle with Kewpie mayo sauce all over to enjoy.
