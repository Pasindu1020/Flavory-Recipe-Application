Chicken Fried Rice

Chicken Fried Rice is a flavorful and satisfying one-pan dish made with tender chicken pieces, fluffy rice, colorful vegetables, and savory seasonings. It's stir-fried to perfection with soy sauce, garlic, and sesame oil, often enriched with scrambled eggs for extra texture and protein. This popular comfort food is quick to prepare and perfect for using up leftover rice, making it a go-to meal for busy weeknights. Whether served as a side or main dish, Chicken Fried Rice delivers a delicious balance of taste and texture in every bite.

Prep Time

15 mins

Cook Time

15 mins

Total Time

30 mins

Ingredients

  • Cooked brown rice or white rice
  • Chicken (boneless, skinless, cut into bite-size pieces) – boneless chicken thighs or lean chicken breasts
  • Vegetable oil (divided use) or coconut oil
  • 1 Egg, scrambled
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • Carrots, chopped
  • Frozen peas
  • Low sodium soy sauce (or more to taste)
  • Toasted sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sliced green onions (for garnish)

Directions

  1. First, in a large skillet, heat 2 teaspoons of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and sauté until browned and cooked through, about 4-5 minutes. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  2. Next, remove the chicken from the pan and place it on a plate. Cover with foil to keep warm. Then, wipe out the pan with a paper towel.
  3. Heat the remaining teaspoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the onion and carrot to the pan, and cook for 3-5 minutes or until softened.
  5. Add the rice to the pan, along with the reserved chicken, egg, and frozen peas. Stir in the soy sauce and sesame oil and mix gently until everything is thoroughly combined.
  6. Finally, season to taste with salt and pepper and top with sliced green onions if desired. Serve.
