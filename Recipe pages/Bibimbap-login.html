Bibimbap

Experience the vibrant flavors of Korea with this classic Bibimbap! A delicious bowl that brings together tender marinated steak, fresh vegetables, and a perfectly fried egg over steaming short-grain rice. Drizzled with a savory soy and mirin sauce and topped with toasted sesame seeds, Bibimbap is a customizable, colorful, and satisfying meal that’s perfect for any time you crave a wholesome, flavorful bowl. Add your favorite veggies to make it your own!

Prep Time

15 mins

Cook Time

15 mins

Total Time

30 mins

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup Light soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Mirin
  • 1 tsp Sesame oil
  • 1 tsp Toasted sesame seeds, lightly crushed
  • 450g (1 lb) Rib-eye steak (or any tender cut like sirloin, rump, scotch fillet)
  • 4 tbsp Peanut oil
  • 3 Garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp Finely grated ginger
  • 100g Fresh mushrooms, sliced
  • 150g Shredded carrots
  • 150g Baby spinach leaves
  • 50g Bean shoots or pea shoots
  • Cooked short grain rice
  • 1 Egg
  • Kimchi (optional), sliced cucumber, and extra sesame seeds for serving

Gochujang Sauce

  • ¼ cup gochujang paste*
  • 2 tbsp mirin (rice wine) – you can use sweet vinegar as well
  • 2 tsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp sesame seeds

Directions

  1. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, mirin, sesame oil, and crushed sesame seeds. Set aside.
  2. Slice the rib-eye steak thinly against the grain. Marinate the steak in half of the soy sauce mixture for about 10 minutes.
  3. Heat 2 tbsp peanut oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Sauté garlic and ginger until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add the marinated steak and cook for 2-3 minutes until just cooked through. Remove from pan and set aside.
  5. In the same pan, add remaining peanut oil. Quickly sauté mushrooms, carrots, spinach, and bean shoots separately or together until just tender, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Fry the egg sunny side up or as desired.
  7. To assemble, place cooked rice in bowls. Arrange the cooked vegetables and steak on top.
  8. Place the fried egg in the center, drizzle with remaining soy sauce mixture, and garnish with kimchi, sliced cucumber, and extra sesame seeds.
  9. Mix everything together before eating for the full Bibimbap experience!
