Coconut Curry Noodles

This Coconut Curry Noodles dish is a rich, creamy, and flavorful fusion of Asian-inspired ingredients that will warm your soul. Tender chicken, fresh vibrant vegetables, and thick udon noodles are all simmered in a luscious coconut curry sauce made with peanut butter, soy sauce, and aromatic spices. The combination of garlic, ginger, red pepper, and curry powder brings bold flavor, while the creamy coconut milk and peanut butter add depth and silkiness. Topped with crushed peanuts and fresh cilantro, it’s a comforting, satisfying meal perfect for both lunch and dinner. Whether you're craving something spicy, creamy, or just delicious—this dish delivers it all in one bowl.

Prep Time

25 mins

Cook Time

20 mins

Total Time

45 mins

Ingredients

    Chicken

  • 600g Chicken
  • 1 tsp Baking soda
  • 1 tsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Vinegar
  • Salt (to taste)
  • White pepper (to taste)

    • Vegetables

  • 3 tbsp Coconut oil
  • 1 Broccoli crown
  • 1 Red bell pepper
  • 1/2 Red onion
  • Salt (to taste)

    • Coconut Curry

  • 1 tsp Grated ginger
  • 6 Garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tsp Red pepper
  • 2 tsp Curry powder
  • 4 tbsp Peanut butter (heaping)
  • 1 can Coconut milk
  • 1 Chicken bouillon cube (if needed)
  • 1.5 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Oyster sauce
  • 2 tbsp Hot sauce
  • 1 cup Water
  • 4 pcs Udon noodles
  • Crushed peanuts (Garnish)
  • Fresh cilantro (Garnish)

Directions

  1. Slice chicken into thin long strips.
  2. Sprinkle the chicken with baking soda and massage into the chicken. Pour cold water over the chicken and stir to dissolve baking soda. Let sit for approximately 15 minutes then drain the liquid and briefly rinse the chicken with cold water to remove excess baking soda.
  3. Marinate chicken with soy sauce, vinegar, white pepper.
  4. Slice red bell pepper and red onion into thin slices and cut up broccoli.
  5. Heat a wok on high-medium heat for 2-3 minutes then add in 1-2 tbsp of any high smoke point oil. Allow about 30 seconds for the oil to get hot.
  6. Toss chopped vegetables into the wok, season lightly with salt and stir-fry on high heat for 2-4 minutes. For crunchier veggies, cook a little less and for softer veggies, cook a little longer. Remove veggies from the pan once cooked to your liking.
  7. In the same pan, add another 1-2 tbsp if needed and fry up the chicken. This should take 3-6 minutes depending on how thinly the chicken is sliced. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the pan.
  8. Next, sauté minced garlic, ginger, red pepper powder, and curry powder for 30-60 seconds.
  9. To the pan, add peanut butter, coconut milk, chicken bouillon (if needed), soy sauce, oyster sauce, vinegar, hot sauce, and water. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.
  10. Bring the curry peanut sauce up to a simmer then add in udon noodles and cooked chicken. Cover and simmer on medium heat for about 3-5 minutes or until udon noodles are cooked according to package instructions. Add the veggies in the last minute of cooking and stir everything to combine.
  11. Garnish with crushed peanuts and lime wedges for extra acidity.
